## Abstract
This post serve as a note for the Unity UI toolkit sample project--Dragon Crashers. it's meant to explains the architecture of that project and standards used, and propose some advice based on my practice. All of these are meant to be helpful when your projects scale up, and you want to keep things organized and extensible. 

Learn about the offical example

# Introduction
Dragon Crashers is the UI Toolkit sample project, demonstrates how you can leverage UI Toolkit for your own applications.

This sample project is available on the [Unity Asset Store](https://assetstore.unity.com/packages/essentials/tutorial-projects/ui-toolkit-sample-dragon-crashers-231178?utm_source=demand-gen&utm_medium=pdf&utm_campaign=authoring-optimizing-ui&utm_content=ui-design-ebook), and introduced in the e-book [User interface design and implementation in Unity](https://resources.unity.com/games/user-interface-design-and-implementation-in-unity?ungated=true). I recommend you take a look at it before reading this note.

The e-book mainly explained what the sample project consists of, while this note take a step father to summarize how things should be organized and give a general idea about how to apply these standards to your project.
